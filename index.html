The Classes

What sets STAYBAD PARAMOTOR apart? It’s STAYBAD’s one-to-one class format and individually tailored training program. In this private setting, you will learn to fly at your own pace and I will target my teaching methods to your specific needs, such as your level of fitness, optimal learning style, schedule, and personal goals. STAYBAD’s one-to-one instruction differs from the more usual “boot camp style”, a group setting in which you attempt to learn all of the required flying skills in about 8 days. For many students, this type of concentrated instruction feels overwhelming and they return home lacking confidence. STAYBAD trainings are scheduled to correspond to your free time (weekdays or weekends). At your own pace, you will get all the theory and practice you need to fly safely. Most beginning students are flying by their 4th lesson.

You will complete the USPPA syllabus and will receive all your ratings: PPG1, PPG2, and PPG3. Your competence will be enhanced by training in varied locations and under diverse flying conditions. Most importantly, you can take classes as long as you need/want to, so when you are done training you will have EVERY skill necessary to be confident flying solo and flying anywhere. Our advanced training offers USPPA Instructor and Tandem Instructor ratings.

Training From A-Z

At STAYBAD PARAMOTOR we make it simple. Our ALL-INCLUSIVE training package is made up of 4 stages:

Ground School (paramotor equipment and theory, weather safety, airspace, etc.)
Kiting (Learning basic glider control, Forward and reverse launch.)
Power Management (Learning how to get the motor on and off your back, how to handle the power and work on posture)
Taxiing (We combine the wing and the motor on your back)

After you master these 4 skill sets, you will be ready for your first solo flight.

All Inclusive Training

What does an all-inclusive training package mean? Simple: it means no hidden fees and no surprises. It includes EVERYTHING you need to get to your first flight safely.

Training Gear

What kind of training gear do you use? We offer multiple gear options, which vary in weight from xx-xx pounds (having small sizes is especially important for smaller, lower weight trainees). Gear examples include Sky Engines, Miniplane, Niviuk, and Ozone.

Flexible Schedule

How flexible is the class schedule? Extremely! We will work with your schedule to find convenient days and times for lessons. And, we will tailor the length of each class to your learning pace and physical endurance—everyone’s different!

Multiple Locations

Where do you train? We use multiple locations throughout Southern California, specifically selected to teach skills in varied conditions and terrain.

A* Customer Service

What about customer service? We will take care of you from A to Z…from helping you to gearing up properly and safely, to helping with engine maintenance, to obtaining any needed warranties, etc.

Rating & License

Do I get a license? There is no legal requirement for a license to paramotor. We will certify your skills by providing you with the USPPA appropriate rating. Rating levels include PPG 1, 2, 3 and Tandem Exemption.
